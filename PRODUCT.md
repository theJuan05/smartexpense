# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Young professionals, students, and families who want to get a handle on their money without needing to be finance experts. They use the app in short mobile sessions — a quick expense log after lunch, a budget check before a weekend purchase — and occasionally for deeper weekly or monthly reviews. They are often anxious or uncertain about their finances; they need the app to feel like a supportive tool, not an authority that judges them.

## Product Purpose

SmartExpense AI Pro helps everyday users track expenses, set budgets, and understand their financial health through AI-assisted insights, receipt scanning, anomaly detection, and a financial health score. Success looks like a user who logs consistently, understands where their money goes, and feels more in control over time — not just a user who has data.

## Brand Personality

Approachable, motivating, clear. The voice is warm but competent — like a financially-savvy friend, not a bank. Progress should feel rewarding. Complexity should be earned, never dumped.

## Anti-references

- **Quickbooks / SAP / enterprise finance tools** — corporate gray, dense forms, no visual warmth
- **Bloomberg / old Mint** — overwhelming data walls that make users feel behind, not informed
- **Aggressive crypto / trading aesthetics** — neon on black, ticker anxiety, speculative energy
- Generic bank app blandness — sterile white with navy headers, zero personality

## Design Principles

1. **Encourage, don't alarm.** Every screen nudges users forward. Negative data (overspending, anomalies) should inform and motivate — never shame. Framing and tone matter as much as the numbers.
2. **Progress is the product.** Health scores, budget bars, and streaks exist to make momentum visible and satisfying. Design should amplify the feeling of getting better over time.
3. **One primary thing per surface.** Each tab has one job. Users should never have to decide what to look at first. Duet information — supporting context is present but subordinate.
4. **Earn complexity.** Show the simple view first. Power features (anomaly detail, AI advice breakdowns, export options) are available but not front-and-center. Families and students shouldn't be confronted with every data point at once.
5. **Mobile-first, always.** The primary context is a quick interaction on a phone in a real-world moment. Desktop is a bonus, not the primary design target.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA as the minimum bar. Both light and dark modes must meet contrast requirements independently — dark mode is a first-class feature, not an afterthought. Reduced motion preference should suppress decorative animations. Color is never the sole indicator of status (budget alerts, sync state, health score tiers should pair color with a label, icon, or shape).
